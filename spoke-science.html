🔬 Spoke 1 — The Science

Your Market Isn't Unique.
Its Physics Are.

A standard for the non-standard.

Every thin market — from specialty grain to rare expertise — suffers from the same predictable frictions: too few participants, too little trust, too much information density. Nobel laureate Alvin Roth identified the problem. DeeperPoint mapped the patterns. AI provides the engineering.

The Market Physics Angle

Most markets fail because they fight the gravity of thinness

Thin markets aren't random. They arise when predictable forces — information density, trust deficits, geographic distance, temporal distance, and cognitive overload — interact to prevent willing participants from finding each other.

DeeperPoint's research identifies a finite taxonomy of these forces. When you know the taxonomy, you can diagnose any thin market and prescribe the right engineering interventions — just as a structural engineer diagnoses loads and designs supports.

The Scaling Complexity Angle

Finally, a standard for the non-standard

The old tradeoff: you can have scale (Amazon, commodity exchanges) or you can have uniqueness (one-off M&A negotiations, bespoke consulting). You couldn't have both.

AI dissolves this tradeoff. Semantic matching uses vector embeddings — mathematical representations in high-dimensional space — to match complex, unique items to fuzzy user intent without forcing standardization. You get the liquidity of a thick market while preserving the information richness of each individual offering.

Think of it as the shipping container for information-rich trade: a benign standard that makes interoperability possible without destroying what makes each offering unique.
