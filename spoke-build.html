The Digital Twin Angle

Stress-test your market physics before you commit

Most marketplace founders learn what doesn't work by watching real users fail. That's expensive and demoralizing. A Digital Twin lets you simulate your market's behavior before launch.

Cosolvent provides the marketplace scaffolding — matching engines, deal assembly, role-based permissions, handoff artifact generation. ClientSynth populates it with a synthetic population of plausible users: buyers, sellers, and intermediaries with realistic profiles, preferences, and constraints.

Run the simulation. Watch where deals stall. Diagnose the friction. Re-engineer. Test again. Then — and only then — recruit real participants into a marketplace that's already been proven to work.

The Global Expansion Angle

Bridge the Saskatchewan-to-Singapore gap instantly

A Canadian barley exporter has never sold to a mill in the Philippines. An Ethiopian coffee cooperative has no pathway to Korean roasters. These aren't hypothetical scenarios — they're the real, daily consequences of thin trade corridors.

Asynchronous brokerage — AI agents that represent participants 24/7 across time zones — bridges temporal distance. Semantic matching handles the information density. Trusted intermediation resolves the trust deficit.

Cosolvent provides the open-source infrastructure to "thicken" trade corridors with partners who are currently out of reach. Middle powers — Canada, the EU, CPTPP nations, and others — are urgently building these corridors. The infrastructure is what's missing.
