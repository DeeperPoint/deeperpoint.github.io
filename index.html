The Idea

Some markets fail — not for lack of supply or demand, but because of friction.

Nobel laureate Alvin Roth identified thin markets as a fundamental economic problem: markets where transactions are infrequent, matching is difficult, and beneficial exchanges fail to occur despite willing participants on both sides.

The DeeperPoint framework identifies two categories of forces that prevent markets from working: existential threats (risk, trust, regulation) that can prevent a market from forming at all, and resistance challenges (opacity, offering complexity, distance, cognitive overload) that reduce efficiency.

For centuries, overcoming these forces required a painful tradeoff: standardize to create thickness (destroying useful information) or preserve uniqueness to maintain relevance (fragmenting markets). AI dissolves this tradeoff.

The Toolkit

Four interconnected initiatives

Each tool addresses a different piece of the thin market puzzle. Together, they form the MarketForge ecosystem.

⚙️

Cosolvent

Open Source · MIT

Composable marketplace scaffolding framework. Matching engine, deal assembly, role-based permissions, handoff artifacts. Build a functioning marketplace in days instead of months.

👥

ClientSynth

Testing Tool

Generate realistic synthetic populations of marketplace participants for testing and demonstration. Stress-test your market physics before committing real resources to user recruitment.

📚

KnowledgeSlot

Knowledge Management

AI-curated domain knowledge management. Captures, structures, and maintains the expertise that marketplace operation requires — from industry standards to regulatory requirements.

🔧

MarketForge

Platform

The integration layer that combines all three tools into deployable, sponsor-ready marketplace platforms. Where the science becomes working software.
