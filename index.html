The Insight

Thin markets have predictable physics

Nobel laureate Alvin Roth identified it: some markets are too thin to function. Too few participants, too little trust, too much complexity — beneficial exchanges fail to occur despite willing participants on both sides. DeeperPoint's research shows these failures fall into a finite set of known patterns. And for the first time, AI can engineer around them.
